## OWASP Committees Policy

### Purpose
This policy defines how OWASP Committees are formed, governed, empowered, and dissolved. Committees are standing working bodies responsible for delivering mission‑aligned outcomes within an approved charter.

### Formation
Committees may be proposed after open discussion in public OWASP leadership channels.

Requirements:

- Public discussion of the proposal.
- A Charter drafted by 3-5 OWASP members, with 3–5 officers designated.
- A Board sponsor to introduce the motion.
- At least one officer present at the establishing Board meeting.
- Executive Director review of membership and charter compliance.

### Charter Requirements
A Charter must include:

- Purpose
- Scope (mission‑aligned objectives)
- Initial officers (3–5 OWASP members)

Charters must:

- Align with OWASP’s mission and Board strategy.
- Avoid overlap with other Committees unless approved.
- Use Foundation infrastructure where possible.
- Be published for community comment 30 days before Board vote.

Charters must not:

- Grant financial or signing authority.
- Bypass policies, controls, or operational requirements.
- Conflict with the [Code of Conduct](https://policy.owasp.org/operational/code-of-conduct) or Board oversight.

Amendments:  
Require a 2/3 officer vote and Board approval.

### Committee Officers
Committees must have 3–5 officers, preferably an odd number.

Required roles:

- Chair – chairs meetings, primary Foundation contact, breaks ties.
- Vice‑Chair – acts in Chair’s absence.
- Secretary – agendas, scheduling, minutes, website updates.

Restrictions:

- Officers must maintain OWASP membership.
- Officers may not simultaneously serve as officers of another Committee, the Global Board, or Foundation staff.

### Staff Liaison
The Foundation may assign a staff liaison to support Committee operations on a best‑effort basis.

### Empowerment & Responsibilities
Committees may:

- Plan and execute initiatives within their charter scope.
- Provide operational governance.
- Recommend policy or process changes.

Committees may not:

- Sign contracts or partnerships.
- Spend outside approved budgets.
  
Operational expectations:

- Establish an annual outcomes calendar.
- Report quarterly progress to the Board.

### Voting
- Only officers vote.
- Majority for standard decisions.
- 2/3 for Charter amendments.
- Votes must be recorded and published.

### Term Limits & Elections
- Officer terms follow [Board Director term durations and limits](https://policy.owasp.org/legal/bylaws#section-43-nomination-election-and-term-of-office-of-directors).
- Elections occur concurrently with Board elections.
- If no new nominees appear, officers may continue subject to Board review.

### Inactivity & Dissolution
A Committee is considered inactive if it fails to:

- Hold at least four public meetings per year or exceeds 120 days between meetings.
- Submit two consecutive quarterly progress reports.
- Submit an annual budget.
- Maintain officer responsiveness.

Process:

1. Foundation notifies Committee of inactivity.
2. Committee has 30 days to correct issues.
3. If unresolved, leadership may be declared vacant and recruitment initiated.
4. If recruitment fails or inactivity persists, the Committee is dissolved.
5. Scope returns to the Board or Foundation.

### Mismanagement
The Board may remove officers or dissolve a Committee for:

- Code of Conduct violations.
- Governance or financial abuse.
- Regulatory violations.
- Transparency failures.
- Repeated failure to meet mission outcomes.

Vacated officers may not serve again for five years.

### Transparency
Committees must:

- Publish meeting agendas publicly.
- Maintain minutes or recordings on the OWASP website.

### Awards, Grants, and Scholarships
Committees may use these programs but must:

- Publish criteria publicly.
- Evaluate applicants transparently.
- Avoid self‑dealing.

All awards and grants are public record.

### Fiduciary Duty
Officers must act in the best interests of OWASP and may request access to Board training materials.

### Finances
Annual Budget
- Committees must submit an annual budget (zero‑dollar budgets allowed).
- Failure to submit removes access to expenses, travel, grants, and scholarships.

Donations & Sponsorships
- Committees may solicit donations and sponsorships.
- All funds must be processed through the Foundation.

Expenses
- Require Chair + one officer approval.
- Must include receipt and purpose.
- All expenses are public record.

Travel
- Must follow OWASP travel guidelines.
- Requires Chair + one officer approval.
- Planned travel should be budgeted as grants.

### Misconduct & Conflict Resolution
Committee members must follow the [Code of Conduct](https://policy.owasp.org/operational/code-of-conduct).

Complaints follow the [Whistleblower process](/operational/whistleblower).

Internal disputes escalate:

1. Committee
2. Executive Director
3. Board

After mediation, the decision of the Compliance Team, Executive Director, or Board is final and binding.
